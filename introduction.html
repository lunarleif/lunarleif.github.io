RuPaul's Drag Race is an American reality competition television series that has captured the hearts of millions of viewers worldwide. As the host and creator of the show, I am proud to say that it has become a global phenomenon, showcasing the fierce talent and creativity of drag performers from all walks of life.

The show first premiered in 2009 and has since gained a massive following, with numerous spin-offs and international adaptations. Each season, a group of talented drag queens compete in a series of challenges that put their skills to the test, from designing their own runway looks to performing in lip sync battles. Along the way, they are mentored and judged by a panel of industry professionals, with one queen ultimately being crowned as the winner and earning the title of America's Next Drag Superstar.

But Drag Race is more than just a competition. It has become a platform for LGBTQ+ visibility and representation, showcasing the diversity and resilience of the community. Through the art of drag, these performers are able to express themselves in ways that defy societal norms and celebrate their individuality.

I am thrilled to see the impact that Drag Race has had on the world, and I am grateful for the opportunity to give these performers a platform to shine. It is my hope that the show will continue to inspire and empower people of all backgrounds to embrace their own unique identities and pursue their dreams with passion and pride.
